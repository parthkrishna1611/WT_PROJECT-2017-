Hip Hop

Hip Hop


Origins:


In the 1970s, an underground urban movement known as "hip hop" began to develop in the South Bronx in New York City. Hip hop dance has been a powerful medium for protesting the impact of legal institutions on minorities, particularly police and prisons.


About the Dance:


Popping is based on the technique of quickly contracting and relaxing muscles to cause a jerk in a dancer's body, referred to as a pop or a hit. When performed correctly, each hit is synchronized to the rhythm and beats of the music.Locking looks similar to popping, and the two are frequently confused by the casual observer. In locking, dancers hold their positions longer.Breaking involves stylish poses done on your hands; and power moves, complex and impressive acrobatic moves.


Benefits:


Hip hop dance is especially good for you aerobically because most hip hop dance classes require you to stay moving and active for up to an hour. During that entire time, your body is taking in oxygen, transporting it to your muscles, and helping you to burn fat in the process.It can also work anaerobically to help you strengthen your muscles, too.
